Is the lab tending toward higher / more consistent profit? The north-star is the one number to watch — you want the line going up.

North-star over time

North-star = our best deployable strategy's profit per game minus half its worst game. It rewards profit and penalizes big crashes, so a rising line means we're finding edges that are both more profitable and safer. Backtest-based — treated as provisional until enough real games confirm it.

What the numbers mean

North-star
Best strategy's per-game profit minus ½·its worst game. Higher = more money and smaller crashes. The headline.
Backtest → live decay
How much worse the strategy does on real upcoming games vs the backtest. Near 0 = the edge is real; big positive = overfit to the past.
Real games tested
Out-of-sample games the strategy has actually traded. We don't trust any edge until ~100 — everything below that is provisional.
Leading strategy
Which strategy currently has the best risk-adjusted score. This can change as the loop iterates.
